# ⚡ Liquid Builder Tables: Operations Manual

### 🎯 Core Philosophy & Values
* **The Value:** Find your true work buddy. Access a dedicated group for hyper-focused brainstorming with peers transparently committed to showing up.
* **The Expectation:** Total commitment and reliable availability. 
* **The ROI:** Your Table is a high-yield investment. The commitment is your availability and buy-in for 14 days. The return is a "Voucher"—proving you are a worthy work buddy and trusted contact.
* **Zero Casual Chat:** These are execution squads, not networking lounges. Casual chatting belongs in open community channels. Table channels are strictly for serious work and results.
* **Urgency Driven:** This is not about "finding time." Only join a Table if your project is so urgent and high-priority that you would be building it anyway, even without a group. Personal sense of urgency is key requirement.
* **Decentralized Identity:** We are not associated with the organization or the channel. We are associated strictly with our WORK.

---

### 📐 Structural Guardrails
* **Length:** Exactly 2-week sprints.
* **Size:** 3 to 5 people maximum.
* **Dissolution by Default:** Every squad automatically dissolves on Day 14. It will never be renewed unless there is an explicitly documented, high-stakes reason to continue.
* **Topic Proposal:** Every table requires a submitted:
  1. Topic Name (e.g., Agentic Browsing Agent)
  2. Concrete Outcome (e.g., A deployed Python script running at <$0.01 per browse)

* **Bring your best self in:** Structure is on purpose leightweight and just guardrails the rest you and your team need to fill out with your personal qualities. If it doesnt work out it anyway disolves at the end of sprint and you can mix and match until you find your people.

---

### 🔄 The Operating Rhythm

#### 🛠️ Daily: The Breadcrumb Feed & Cross-Pollination
* **No Mandatory Standups:** Do not schedule sync calls or write corporate status reports.
* **Drop Your Breadcrumbs:** Whenever you finish a work session, drop a 1-to-2 sentence raw update in the channel. State exactly where you stopped and what code/file you are touching next. 
* **The Context Habit:** Read your teammates' breadcrumbs before you start your own work session. This ensures you know exactly how their activity relates to yours.
* **The Flare Gun Rule:** If a breadcrumb turns into a hard stop, tag it `🚨 UNBLOCK`. Because everyone reads the daily breadcrumbs, the team already has the context to jump in and solve it within 24 hours.

#### 💓 Friday Heartbeat: Team-Level Synthesis (Collaborative)
* **The Rule:** Individuals do not post solo updates. The Table must co-author and submit ONE single, unified Heartbeat update to the community by Friday at 21:00.
* **The Process:** Use your daily Breadcrumbs as the raw ingredients. On Friday afternoon, discuss, debate, and synthesize your collective insights. 
* **The Focus:** Don't just list tasks. Focus heavily on Question 2 (What did we learn?) and Question 4 (What collaboration patterns worked?). This is where the true team intelligence is built.

      1. What did we do?
      2. What did we learn? ⭐️
      3. What is blocked?
      4. Any noteworthy collaboration pattern that worked unusually well? ⭐️

#### 📦 Sprint End: Public Artefacts
Every Table must ship their results via two distinct bulletins:

📌 1. [ Liquid Builder Table Post Bulletin ]
* Summary: 2–3 sentences on what the Table built.
* Link: Direct link to the hosted artifact (GitHub, Notion, Figma, etc.).
* Contributors: @User1, @User2, @User3 (Public credit).

🧪 2. [ Lab Intelligence Bulletin ]
* Epic Wins Only: 1 bullet point detailing the exact tool, framework, or workflow hack that made this specific squad move incredibly fast.
* Example: "Using shared Loom videos instead of design reviews cut our alignment time in half."
* Contributors: @User1, @User2, @User3.
